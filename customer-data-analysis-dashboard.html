Executive Summary

Complete Findings & Strategic Recommendations

This comprehensive analysis of 29,694 customers across 742 business practices reveals distinct customer segments with varying lifetime values and tenure patterns. By conducting this data analysis we have drawn valuable insights into our customers that can help us to adjust our strategy and target our marketing purposefully. We firstly addressed the right-hand censoring in customer lifetime calculations using Kaplan-Meier survival analysis to obtain accurate lifetime averages. We analysed client attributes; company type, business practices and acquisition methods to determine the highest lifetime value customer profiles to target. Other interesting trends were found such as the inverse relationship between revenue per policy and customer tenure: medical practices generate high revenue (£140-160/policy) but shorter tenure (2 years), while professional and consulting practices offer lower revenue (£50-70/policy) but exceptional longevity (6 years).

Key Strategic Findings
Critical Methodological Discovery

Nearly 50% of customers are still active (right-censored data). Using Kaplan-Meier survival analysis reveals the true customer lifetime is 5.88 years, not the 2.77 years from naive calculations - a 52% underestimation that would severely undervalue the business.

Revenue Pattern Discovery

Inverse relationship identified: Medical practices generate high revenue (£140-160/policy) with shorter tenure (2 years), while consulting practices offer lower revenue (£50-70/policy) but exceptional longevity (6 years). This suggests two viable business models for portfolio optimization.

Customer Type Performance

Firms deliver 38% higher lifetime value than Individuals (£273 vs £198) despite comprising only 13.8% of the customer base. This B2B segment shows superior retention (57.1% vs 48.0%) and commands premium pricing.
